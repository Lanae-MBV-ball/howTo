How To Read The Bible

Introduction

The Bible is the basis of every faithful Christian's life. Christianity is challenging, selfless, and forgiving. If you are doubting your faith or going through a hard time, these steps for reading the Bible may help.

Steps Helpful Verses Helpful Chapters Helpful Resources Conclusions

Steps

Follow the steps below to learn how to read the Bible effectively and find some inspirational verses and chapters.

  1. Start by picking up your Bible (a trusted version I use is ESV, but I also cross-reference other translations). Flip to a random page and read a verse or a whole chapter. You might not immediately find something that speaks to you, and that's okay.
  2. Use a plan to keep focused on your need. You could also look up 'Bible verses to help with ___' fill in the blank, and the internet will bring up verses other people thought were helpful.
  3. Pray before you read. Ask God to help you understand and apply what you read.
  4. Take notes. Write down any verses that stand out to you, or any thoughts you have while reading.
  5. Keep a journal for your prayers and notes. Writing helps clarify your thoughts and creates a record you can re-read later as you grow in your faith.
  6. Apply what you read. Try to live out the lessons from the Bible in your daily life. Remember that everyone makes mistakes â€” what matters is how you respond and grow.
  7. Be consistent. Make reading the Bible a regular part of your routine, whether it's daily, weekly, or whatever works for you at first. After a while try to do more and more everyday.
  8. Join a Bible study or small group for support, accountability, and different perspectives on scripture. If your perspective changes, that's part of growing.
  9. Don't be afraid to ask questions. If you encounter something unclear, seek answers from trusted mentors, pastors, or reputable resources, and pray for wisdom.
  10. Remember that reading the Bible is a journey. It's okay if you don't understand everything right away, or even ever. Keep reading, praying, and seeking God, and He will reveal His plan for you in His timing.

Helpful Verses

  • Deuteronomy 31:8, "It is the LORD who goes before you. He will be with you; he will not forsake you. Do not fear or be dismayed."
  • Joshua 1:9, "Have I not commanded you? Be strong and courageous. Do not be frightened, and do not be dismayed, for the LORD your God is with you wherever you go."
  • Psalm 23:4, "Even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil, for you are with me; your rod and your staff, they comfort me."
  • Isaiah 41:10, "Fear not, for I am with you; be not dismayed, for I am your God; I will strengthen you, I will help you, I will uphold you with my righteous right hand."
  • Matthew 11:28, "Come to me, all who labor and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest."
  • 1 Peter 5:7, "Casting all your anxieties on him, because he cares for you."

Helpful Chapters

  • Psalm 23 - Let the LORD be your shepherd.
  • Psalm 91 - Psalm of the LORD being your refuge and fortress.
  • Romans 8 - A hard chapter full of theology, but one of my father's favorites.
  • 1 Corinthians 13 - The way of love.
  • Philippians 4 - This whole book is a letter of joy; in particular, this chapter contains encouragement for the Philippians.
  • Hebrews 11 - Faith, always build your Faith.
  • Revelation 21 - The new creation; it gives hope for the future. Revelation can be one of the more challenging books to understand.

Helpful Resources

  1. Bible Gateway - A website that allows you to read the Bible in different translations and languages.
  2. Blue Letter Bible - Provides tools for studying the Bible, including commentaries and concordances.
  3. YouVersion Bible App - A mobile app for reading the Bible, listening to audio versions, and using reading plans.
  4. RightNow Media - Christian study videos on different books of the Bible.

Conclusions

Reading and understanding the Bible is a lifelong journey; it's okay to take it one step at a time. Stay consistent, pray, and be open to what God teaches through His Word. The goal is not just to read the Bible but to let it transform your life and draw you closer to God.
